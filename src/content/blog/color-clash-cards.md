## A World Where Color Matters

[cite_start]It is time to lift the curtain and look straight into the Game Design Document by Velocity Vale Studios for the TurboBlocks server! [cite: 3, 4]

[cite_start]In the near future, color is no longer cosmetic[cite: 10]. [cite_start]It is energy, identity, currency, and a weapon[cite: 11]. [cite_start]Our custom mode, **Color Clash**, represents sanctioned conflicts where competitors fight for dominance in chromatic arenas[cite: 14, 15].

[cite_start]Each match will last between 8 to 15 minutes and host up to 16 players per arena[cite: 6, 7].

## Mechanics: Painting is Control

[cite_start]In Color Clash, painting surfaces is not decoration[cite: 36]. [cite_start]It is absolute control[cite: 37].

Here is what you need to know about survival and elimination:
* [cite_start]Stepping onto an enemy-controlled surface triggers an elimination, visually dissolving the player into particles of their own color[cite: 54, 55].
* [cite_start]There is no time to be bored—respawn is fast and re-entry into the arena is immediate[cite: 56, 57].
* [cite_start]When a player paints continuously without being eliminated, they enter a flow state represented by a combo multiplier, which heavily boosts their scoring output[cite: 46, 47].

## Arsenal and Power-ups

[cite_start]Forget traditional weapons[cite: 61]. [cite_start]Tools in Color Clash are ways to project your presence[cite: 62].
* [cite_start]**Paint Brush**: Represents precision and intent[cite: 63].
* [cite_start]**Paint Cannon**: Built for reach and applying pressure[cite: 64].
* [cite_start]**Paint Roller**: The perfect gear for momentum and expansion[cite: 64].
* [cite_start]**Paint Grenades**: Used to create sudden moments of disruption[cite: 65].

[cite_start]Unstable bursts of condensed chromatic energy, known as power-ups, will also appear unpredictably[cite: 68, 69]. [cite_start]Players are meant to fight over them[cite: 70]. [cite_start]You will find game-changers like Color Shields, Color Rockets, and the massive Neon Turbo Color[cite: 74, 75, 76].

## Where and How Will We Play?

The environment is not static. [cite_start]As chromatic saturation increases, systems activate—platforms move and hazards emerge[cite: 91, 92, 93].

Each map has its own unique character:
* [cite_start]**Neon City**: Engineered dominance[cite: 82].
* [cite_start]**Volcanic Island**: Raw instability[cite: 83].
* [cite_start]**Frozen Realm**: Suppressed energy[cite: 84].
* [cite_start]**Rainbow Maze**: Experimental distortion[cite: 85].

[cite_start]You can test your skills in the individual dominance of **Free-for-All**, coordinated control in **Team Battle**, or dive into the unsanctioned overload of **Chaos Mode**[cite: 98, 99, 100].

[cite_start]Color Clash is not a side attraction—it is the foundation of TurboBlocks[cite: 122, 127]. [cite_start]Get ready for the spectacle! [cite: 131]
